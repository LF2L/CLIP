Climate Labs Incubation Program 2021

Sprint 6 Pilot Project

6.1 Sprint Opening

This is the last sprint of the CLIP. This sprint is a transition from the incubation program to the implementation of the Pilot Project of each Climate Lab.

GOAL

The goal of sprint V is:

To set-up the local Climate Lab pilot project moving from the strategic to the operational level.

Sprint dates: From September 24 to October 15 / 2021

In this sprint the Climate Lab Teams will focus their efforts on the set-up of their pilot project. In this case leaders from the Work Package 2.4 join us to provide the introduce the methodology that will guide the pilot project implementation. Specifically for this sprint, the teams are invited to assess their territory and community.

  1. For this sprint the last two conceptual videos share insights on the application of the living lab approach. This approach/methodology is considered suitable to the pilot projects.

  2. Then, the final team workshop is proposed by the colleagues from Work Package 2.4 during the opening webinar.

6.2 Conceptual Videos

6.2.1 Living Lab approach applied to French forestry policy priorities - Maxence Arnould

Maxence Arnould, from the University of Lorraine, shares his experience and insights on the application of the Living Lab approach to increase the commitment of private forest owners in French forest policy priorities. To know more about this case and the theoretical foundations of this work you can refer to (Arnould, Morel, and Fournier 2021).

6.2.2 Living Lab approach applied to French forestry policy priorities - Maxence Arnould

Laurent Dupont from Université de Lorraine shares in this video the Linky by Makers project as a new form of collaboration between Makers or FabLabs , Academics and Companies. By Sharing ideas and pooling resources, Linky by Makers sought to prototype citizen solutions for “Smart Grid” deployment. To know more about this project you can have a look to the paper (Dupont et al. 2019).

References

Arnould, Maxence, Laure Morel, and Meriem Fournier. 2021. “Developing the Persona Method to Increase the Commitment of Non-Industrial Private Forest Owners in French Forest Policy Priorities.” Forest Policy and Economics 126 (May): 102425. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.forpol.2021.102425.
Dupont, Laurent, Joëlle Mastelic, Nathalie Nyffeler, Sophie Latrille, and Eric Seulliet. 2019. “Living lab as a support to trust for co-creation of value: application to the consumer energy market.” Journal of Innovation Economics 28 (1): 53. https://doi.org/10.3917/jie.028.0053.
