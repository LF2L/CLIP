Mentors

  • Universitat Politècnica de València

Carola Calabuig PhD. Industrial Engineer by the Universitat Politècnica de València (UPV), she is full professor of the Department of Engineering Projects at the UPV and currently Academic Director of the Master’s Degree in Development Cooperation at the UPV, specializing in Project Management and Development Processes. Her main areas of work and interests are framed in development studies and include: development planning; international cooperation; sustainable development and critical approaches; democratic governance; education for development and global citizenship.

Álvaro Fernández-Baldor works at the Center for Development Cooperation of the Polytechnic University of Valencia (UPV - Spain), and is an associate professor in the Department of Engineering Projects of the UPV. He teaches in the Master of Development Cooperation of the UPV. He has done his PhD researching technological projects from the human development and capabilities approach. He also teaches as a guest lecturer in different fields, especially in cooperation masters in the areas related to engineering and development cooperation. He has field experience in Guatemala, Brazil, Peru and Kenya. In 2007 he received the first prize for the best final project in the IV PFC Competition in the field of technology for human development, for a design of an energy supply in an isolated rural area of Guatemala. In 2015 he has been awarded with the Doctoral Thesis Award for Development Cooperation of the Group-9 Universities.

  • Università di Genova

Dr.-Ing. Paola Alfaro d’Alençon just started in February 2018 as a DFG-Research Fellow associated to the TU-Berlin, Faculty VI, Habitat Unit with the research project for the DFG: Concepts of Co-production and its influence on the development of inclusive spaces – An international comparative study on theories and practices”. She has 15 years of national and international work experience in research, education, knowledge transfer, and urban management and planning. These experiences are based on the topics of sustainable urban development associated with projects on housing (resilient development through interdisciplinary approaches linked to water management and local production chains, as well as through local solutions); governance, and co-production, with network development and stakeholder, cooperation management on urban and regional development strategies related to subjects of climate change, transport and mobility, and urban resilience.

  • Ashoka

Waldo Soto is a commercial engineer from the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile with a Diploma in Innovation from Babson College, a Diploma in Global Management from University College London, and a MSc in Urban Management from Technical University Berlin. As Director of 2811, Waldo specialises in social innovation and sustainable urban development.

Gabriela Carrasco is director of 2811 and consultant for Ashoka in Latin America. MPA in Social Innovation and Impact Investment at NYU, Commercial Engineer and Political Scientist at the Catholic University of Chile. Contributing to the solution of the challenges of our region through the articulation of actors and the formation of agents of change.
